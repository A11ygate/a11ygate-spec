**Market framing:** WebAIM 2026 Million report found detectable WCAG failures on 95.9% of home pages tested. WHO estimates 1.3 billion people (16% of global population) experience significant disability. The supply-side failure is structural, not marginal.

**Strategic lane:** Not "scan my website" (low margin, crowded). The richer lane is verification infrastructure: certification, registry, procurement evidence, enterprise remediation workflow, AI interface accessibility governance, public-sector readiness packets.

## Buyer Archetypes

### Primary

**1. The Procurement Gatekeeper**
- Role: Sourcing, vendor management, procurement counsel
- Fear: Buying a tool that creates exposure
- Need: A clean yes/no/conditional record
- Message: "Know which vendors can prove access before the contract is signed."

**2. The In-House Counsel**
- Role: Legal, risk, compliance
- Fear: Overclaiming, weak documentation, repeat complaints
- Need: Scoped evidence, defensible language, exception logs
- Message: "Accessibility claims should be reviewable before they become legal problems."

**3. The Public-Sector Operator**
- Role: City, county, school district, public agency leader
- Fear: Missing DOJ deadlines, inaccessible public services, resident complaints
- Need: Clear remediation plan, budget logic, status record
- Message: "A11yGate turns accessibility into a managed public-service obligation."

**4. The Product Leader**
- Role: VP Product, Head of Design, PM
- Fear: Shipping barriers, embarrassing failures, audit chaos
- Need: Prioritized fixes, severity logic, roadmap integration
- Message: "Find the barriers that block real users before launch."

**5. The Engineering Lead**
- Role: CTO, staff engineer, platform owner
- Fear: Vague accessibility tickets that never resolve
- Need: CI/CD gate, reproducible failures, issue routing
- Message: "Make accessibility testable, assignable, and release-blocking when needed."

### Extended

**6. The Disabled Power User / Advocate**
- Role: Public user, tester, advocate, watchdog
- Fear: Being erased by inaccessible systems
- Need: Proof, challenge path, public accountability
- Message: "Access failures deserve receipts."

**7. The Institutional Reformer**
- Role: Board member, civic tech funder, standards coalition partner
- Fear: Accessibility theater
- Need: New standard, public registry, durable governance
- Message: "A11yGate makes access visible, inspectable, and enforceable."

## Brand Archetypes

- **Primary: The Gatekeeper** — A11yGate decides what passes. By standard, evidence, scope, and review.
- **Secondary: The Witness** — A11yGate records what happened. What failed. What passed. What was fixed. What expired.
- **Tertiary: The Engineer-Judge** — Translates human access into technical, legal, and operational proof.
- **Shadow to avoid: The Scold** — must not become moralizing accessibility theater. Tone: "Here is the barrier. Here is the risk. Here is the fix. Here is the record."

## Regulatory Demand Anchors

- **DOJ Title II (2024):** WCAG 2.1 Level AA required for state/local government web and mobile. Compliance deadline April 24, 2026 (already passed — demand wave active now).
- **Section 508:** ICT accessibility for U.S. federal agencies.
- **European Accessibility Act:** EU-wide requirements for key products and services.
- **WCAG 2.2:** Nine additional success criteria beyond WCAG 2.1 — lets A11yGate position as more current than teams frozen at older checklists.

## Core Strategic Rule

Do not position A11yGate as a generic scan tool. Position it as the **accessibility verification layer for governed systems.**
